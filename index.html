What is Pickleball Pals Society?

Pickleball Pals Society is a non-profit organization. Our Society is passionately committed to advancing the sport of Pickleball in the vibrant community of Greater Vancouver.
As ambassadors for the sport, our mission is to cater to players of all ages, skill levels, and abilities. We host a number of different events throughout the year, ranging from casual to competitive. Our current calendar includes indoor/outdoor recreational play and DUPR rating sessions. In the future, we intend to host tournaments and learn-to-play clinics.

Our Team

George Diewald

George Diewald, a co-founder of Pickleball Pals Society, is a passionate advocate for the sport.
His infectious love for the game is evident as he dedicates himself and is the driving force in expanding and nurturing the Pickleball community through the Non-Profit organization. With a warm welcome extended to participants of all ages and skill levels, George is committed to fostering an inclusive environment within the society.
Although George has a fondness for tennis, Pickleball has captured his heart on a whole new level. The sport's unique blend of challenges, social interactions, and sheer enjoyment has made it a focal point in his life. George actively encourages individuals, both young and old, to join the thriving Pickleball community and experience the joy that comes with it.
In his role within the Pickleball Pals Society, George takes on the responsibility of organizing various play sessions, including DUPR's, Rec Play, and Tournaments. Through these events, he aims to provide opportunities for members to connect, compete, and revel in the fun that Pickleball offers. George's dedication to the growth and vibrancy of the Pickleball Pals Society reflects his unwavering commitment to the sport and the community he is so passionate about.
Hobbies and Interests: Anything outdoors - skiing, fishing, camping, hiking, foraging and traveling.
FUN FACTS: Raises chickens, has 6 cats and traveled throughout the entire France in an RV.

Lucie Gautier

Lucie's pickleball journey began as a personal passion that evolved into a professional pursuit.
She is a proud holder of the Certified Level 1 - Pickleball Instructor designation accredited by the National Coaching Certification Program (NCCP).
Lucie brings a comprehensive understanding of coaching principles and a commitment to fostering a positive and inclusive learning environment. Her coaching philosophy is rooted in the belief that pickleball is a game for everyone, and she is dedicated to sharing her expertise with beginners, youth, and seniors alike.
Lucie's coaching sessions are not just about mastering the technical aspects of pickleball; they are also infused with her infectious love for the sport. She strives to instill the joy of playing pickleball and the satisfaction of continuous improvement in each of her students. Whether you are taking your first steps onto the court or looking to refine your skills, Lucie's coaching style is tailored to meet the unique needs and goals of every individual.
With a warm and encouraging demeanor, Lucie creates an environment where players feel motivated, supported, and excited to learn. Her commitment to coaching extends beyond the court, as she seeks to build a community of pickleball enthusiasts who share in the camaraderie and spirit of the game.
Lucie Gautier is not just a coach; she is a mentor, a motivator, and a guide on your pickleball journey. Join her on the court, and let the love of pickleball unfold in a coaching experience that transcends skill development and becomes a lifelong appreciation for the sport.
Other hobbies and interests include: running, hiking, reading, star-gazing, playing board games and traveling.

Brandon Gautier

Brandon's passion for sports was evident long before he had discovered pickleball. As a child, he would often be found playing soccer and badminton with the other neighborhood children. When he grew older, Brandon focused his sporting efforts on cross-country running. It was no surprise that the moment he stepped onto the pickleball court, he was hooked. Pickleball included aspects from all of his favorite sports - it was challenging like soccer, nuanced like badminton, and competitive like running.
Like most enthusiasts, he wants the sport of pickleball to expand to a wider audience. Brandon enjoys teaching newcomers how to play - so much so, he became a certified instructor with the National Coaching Certification Program (NCCP) in 2023. His coaching sessions focus on making the learning process both fun and informative.
